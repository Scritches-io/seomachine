# Scritches Features & Benefits

This document outlines **Scritches** key features, benefits, and differentiators to inform content creation that drives trial conversions and customer acquisition.

---

## Core Value Propositions

### 1. Online Booking
- **Feature**: Client-facing booking pages with **publicly visible services, pricing, and availability**, plus booking rules and automated confirmations.
- **Feature**: Clients can book **without creating an account** (portal account is optional).
- **Feature**: Providers can embed **intake + agreement signing** directly into the booking flow.
- **Benefit**: Less back-and-forth over texts/emails and fewer drop-offs during the “discovery” phase.
- **Differentiator**: Most competitors hide availability/services behind inquiry flows or login walls.
- **Conversion Angle**: “Let clients see what you offer and request care immediately, without creating yet another account.”

### 2. Booking Landing Pages
- **Feature**: A customizable booking landing page that acts like a **digital profile** for the business.
- **Includes**: Business details, service catalog, pricing, availability calendar, testimonials/reviews.
- **Benefit**: Converts more new leads and reinforces professionalism for existing clients.
- **Differentiator**: A single link that functions as both marketing and booking, without requiring setup across multiple tools.

### 3. Scheduling & Calendar
- **Feature**: Calendar-first scheduling that’s easier to read and use (intended to feel familiar to Google Calendar-style workflows).
- **Feature**: Support for both **manual bookings** and **client-requested bookings**.
- **Feature**: Booking statuses (pending, confirmed, completed, etc.).
- **Benefit**: Clear operational visibility without clunky “industrial” UI.
- **Differentiator**: Scheduling is designed to be legible and fast, not configuration-heavy.

### 4. Nightly/Daily Services (Overnights, Boarding, Daycare)
- **Feature**: Built-in support for services billed on a **nightly/daily basis**, including multi-day logic.
- **Benefit**: Overnights and extended care behave like first-class features rather than workarounds.
- **Differentiator**: Competitors often treat overnights as a hack or require awkward scheduling tricks.

### 5. Client & Pet CRM
- **Feature**: Centralized database for clients, pets, emergency contacts, vets, care notes, and history.
- **Feature**: Internal notes for provider-only context.
- **Benefit**: The right info is always attached to the right client/pet, without searching through old messages.
- **Differentiator**: Designed to feel clean and navigable, not dated or buried in menus.
- **Conversion Angle**: “Everything you know about a client or pet, right where you need it.”

### 6. Forms & Intake (Modular)
- **Feature**: Custom forms that can be used for intake, updates, internal tracking, feedback, inquiries, and more.
- **Feature**: Form submissions automatically populate the CRM and attach to client/pet profiles.
- **Feature**: Forms can be placed in multiple workflows: online booking, portal signup, one-off send links, embedded on a website.
- **Benefit**: Gather the information you actually need without PDFs, email threads, or copying/pasting.
- **Differentiator**: Forms are modular and workflow-flexible instead of forcing you into the platform’s rigid onboarding sequence.
- **Conversion Angle**: “Stop digging through messages for details. Keep everything attached to the client.”

### 7. Digital Agreements & Documents (Modular)
- **Feature**: Upload business documents and policies in one place.
- **Feature**: Collect signatures digitally and place agreements into multiple workflows: booking, portal signup, intake forms, one-off signing links.
- **Benefit**: More consistent policy enforcement and fewer missed signatures, without chasing paperwork.
- **Differentiator**: Agreements are workflow-flexible, not locked to one “onboarding-only” moment.
- **Conversion Angle**: “Collect signed agreements at the right moment, every time.”

### 8. Invoicing
- **Feature**: Automatic invoice creation from bookings.
- **Feature**: Deposits, due dates, and payment status tracking.
- **Benefit**: Less manual math, fewer missed charges, and clearer payment expectations.
- **Differentiator**: Deposit flows are treated as a priority because deposits are becoming a modern industry standard.
- **Conversion Angle**: “Turn bookings into invoices automatically and stay on top of what’s paid.”

### 9. Payments (Stripe + Offline Tracking)
- **Feature**: Stripe credit card payments.
- **Feature**: Track offline payments like Venmo, Zelle, Cash App, PayPal, cash, checks.
- **Benefit**: Providers can support how clients actually pay today, not just credit cards.
- **Differentiator**: Emphasis on modern payment methods, which many pet-sitting tools have not caught up to.

### 10. Automated Surcharges
- **Feature**: Automatic pricing rules for weekends, holidays, extra pets, extended stays, and other edge cases.
- **Benefit**: Pricing stays consistent without mental overhead or forgetting special cases.
- **Conversion Angle**: “Charge correctly every time, without remembering the weird exceptions.”

### 11. Pet Report Cards
- **Feature**: Visit summaries with notes, photos, videos, and optional checklists.
- **Benefit**: Clients feel informed and confident about the care their pets receive.
- **Differentiator**: Report Cards are designed to feel polished and pleasant, reinforcing the provider’s brand.
- **Conversion Angle**: “Turn every visit into a professional update clients love.”

### 12. Review Booster
- **Feature**: Automated review requests after services complete.
- **Targets**: Google, Yelp, Facebook, Nextdoor (provider-controlled destinations).
- **Optional**: Can be integrated into Report Cards to request reviews at a high-satisfaction moment.
- **Benefit**: More reviews with minimal effort, which improves trust and lead conversion.
- **Differentiator**: Review automation is common in facility/grooming platforms, but rare in sitter/walker-focused software.

### 13. Pet Parent Portal (Optional)
- **Feature**: Client portal for viewing bookings, invoices, pets, report cards, and shared documents.
- **Feature**: Portal is available, but not required for core client interactions.
- **Benefit**: Self-service access reduces repetitive questions and improves professionalism.
- **Differentiator**: Scritches treats the portal as a convenience, not a gate. Clients can still access key flows without a login wall.

### 14. Staff Management (Coming Soon)
- **Feature**: Team scheduling and staff visibility designed to match the simplicity of Scritches’ calendar experience.
- **Benefit**: Enables Scritches to move upmarket for teams while preserving usability.
- **Differentiator**: Built to avoid “click day-by-day” staff scheduling experiences common in other tools.

## Competitive Differentiators (Feature-Led)

### Transparent booking experience (client-side)
- Services, pricing, and availability are visible up front.
- No forced account creation to request care.

### Modular workflows (provider-side)
- Forms and agreements can be used in multiple contexts, not locked to a single onboarding funnel.

### Designed for modern expectations
- Clean UI that feels current.
- Supports how clients actually pay (Venmo, Zelle, etc.).
- Deposit flows treated as a first-class billing behavior.

### Built for solo + small teams (not enterprise)
- Fast setup without long onboarding or training.
- Flexible workflows without feature bloat.

## Common Objections (Feature-Based Answers)

### “Do my clients need an account?”
No. Clients can request bookings, sign agreements, and complete forms without creating an account. The portal is optional.

### “Can I still do things manually?”
Yes. Scritches supports manual workflows alongside automation so providers stay in control.

### “What if I already have clients?”
You can add existing clients and bookings any time, and use Scritches in phases rather than switching everything at once.

## Language Guardrails for Feature Content
When describing features and benefits:

- Avoid pet-pun overload (“pawsitively,” “pawsome day”).
- Avoid corporate SaaS jargon (“streamline operations,” “end-to-end solution”).
- Avoid grand claims (“revolutionizing,” “disrupting”).
- Avoid obviously AI-sounding copy and generic competitor phrases.
- Use specific, grounded examples (booking transparency, deposit collection, login-wall avoidance).
- Keep CTAs calm and practical (trial, demo, “try with one client”).

## Quick “Before vs After” Examples (Feature Framing)

### Lead discovery + booking
- **Before**: Clients hunt for services and pricing, then email/text to check availability, then get pushed into a login wall.
- **After (Scritches)**: Clients can see services, pricing, and availability immediately, then request care without an account.

### Intake + agreements
- **Before**: Separate tools (forms, PDFs, signature apps) and manual copying into records.
- **After (Scritches)**: Forms and agreements are integrated and attach directly to client/pet profiles.

### Payments
- **Before**: Manual invoices and payment tracking across Venmo/Zelle/cash plus spreadsheets.
- **After (Scritches)**: Invoices are generated from bookings, Stripe is available, and offline payments are tracked in the same system.
